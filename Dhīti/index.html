Tulsi

(Ocimum basilicum)

Tulsi is originated in north central India and now grows native throughout the eastern world tropics.[2] Within Ayurveda, tulsi is known as “The Incomparable One,” “Mother Medicine of Nature” and “The Queen of Herbs,” and is revered as an “elixir of life” that is without equal for both its medicinal and spiritual properties.

Yoga

Regular consumption of tulsi tea may be compared with the regular practice of yoga, which can be considered “adaptogenic” through nurturing and nourishing the body -- mind -- spirit while fostering a sense of relaxation and wellbeing

Divine

In Hinduism, tulsi is worshipped as a goddess and every part of the tulsi plant is revered and considered sacred, Tulsi is further integrated into daily life through evening and morning rituals and other spiritual and purification practice that can involve ingesting its leaves or consuming tulsi tea.
